Here are a few highlights of things I’ve done:

Data Ethics

  • Currently Professor of Practice at Queensland University of Technology Centre for Data Science
  • Founding director of the Center for Applied Data Ethics (CADE) at the University of San Francisco
  • The Problem with Metrics is a Fundamental Problem for AI, paper accepted to Ethics of Data Science Conference 2020. Optimizing a given metric is a central aspect of most current AI approaches, yet overemphasizing metrics leads to manipulation, gaming, and a myopic short-term focus.
  • Medicine’s Machine Learning Problem, article published in Boston Review. Biased datasets and unaccountable algorithms threaten to further disempower patients.
  • Created and taught data ethics course on bias, surveillance, disinformation, practical tools, algorithmic colonialism, and more.
  • Wrote ethics chapter of best-selling book, Deep Learning for Coders with Fastai and PyTorch: AI Applications Without a PhD
  • Essay “Algorithms are Used Differently than Human Decision Makers” included in the book 97 Things About Ethics Everyone in Data Science Should Know
  • Getting Specific About Algorithmic Bias, featured talk at PyBay 2019
  • Organized CADE Tech Policy Workshop with policy makers, academics, activists, and those who work in industry, all videos are available online
  • The Far-Reaching Impact of Dr. Timnit Gebru, published in The Gradient, was their 2nd most read article of 2020

Machine Learning and Data Science

  • Co-founder of course.fast.ai (2016-present), the most widely-used deep learning course in the world. The Economist featured the course in New schemes teach the masses to build AI – Treating it like a craft is paying dividends
  • Designed and taught graduate level courses on Natural Language Processing and Computational Linear Algebra in the USF Masters of Data Science program
  • The New Era in NLP, Keynote at SciPy (Scientific Python) Conference 2019
  • Lessons Learned from Helping 200,000 non-ML experts use ML, Keynote at ICML AutoML workshop, based on my popular series of AutoML posts
  • Beginner friendly workshop on Word Embeddings (such as Word2Vec), 2017
  • Selected by Forbes as one of 20 Incredible Women in AI, 2017
  • Featured in the book Women Tech Founders on the Rise
  • Early data scientist and software engineer at Uber (2013-2014)
  • Earned PhD in Mathematics from Duke University

Diversity and Inclusion in Tech

  • Artificial Intelligence needs all of us, TEDx talk I gave to an audience of 1,000 people
  • If you think women in tech is just a pipeline problem, you haven’t been paying attention, my viral blog post covering the research on why (and how many) women leave the tech industry
  • The Real Reason Women Quit Tech (and How to Address It)
  • Coordinated diversity scholarships and international fellowships for fast.ai. Increasing diversity and breaking down cliquishness of AI have been core to our fast.ai mission from the start
