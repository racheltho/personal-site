Rachel Thomas, PhD
SearchTags

Data Ethics

  • Founding director of the Center for Applied Data Ethics (CADE) at the University of San Francisco
  • The Problem with Metrics is a Fundamental Problem for AI paper accepted to Ethics of Data Science Conference 2020. Optimizing a given metric is a central aspect of most current AI approaches, yet overemphasizing metrics leads to manipulation, gaming, a myopic focus on short-term goals, and other unexpected negative consequences.
  • Medicine’s Machine Learning Problem published in Boston Review. As Big Data tools reshape health care, biased datasets and unaccountable algorithms threaten to further disempower patients.
  • Created and taught data ethics course on bias, surveillance, disinformation, practical tools, algorithmic colonialism, and more.
  • Wrote ethics chapter of best-selling Deep Learning for Coders with Fastai and PyTorch: AI Applications Without a PhD book
  • Getting Specific About Algorithmic Bias featured talk at PyBay 2019

Machine Learning and Data Science

  • Designed and taught graduate level courses on natural language processing and computational linear algebra in the University of San Francisco Masters of Data Science program
  • Keynote: The New Era in NLP at SciPy (Scientific Python) Conference 2019
  • Keynote: Lessons Learned from Helping 200,000 non-ML experts use ML from the AutoML workshop at ICML 2019, based on my popular series of AutoML posts
  • Beginner friendly workshop on Word Embeddings (such as Word2Vec), 2017
  • Early data scientist and software engineer at Uber (2013-2014)
  • Earned PhD in Mathematics from Duke University (2010)

Diversity and Inclusion in Tech
